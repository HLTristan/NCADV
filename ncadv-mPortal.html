From NCADV

Legislative Guide

NCADV's legislative guide is a user-friendly introduction to engaging with your Members of Congress. It includes an overview of the legislative process and information about connecting directly with your Members of Congress and their staff, effectively using traditional and social media to influence your members of Congress, engaging in community activism and building coalitions. It also contains a variety of helpful example letters, scripts and other materials based on real legislation from the 114th Congress.

Get Guide

 

Remember My Name Poster

NCADV, in conjunction with Ms. Magazine, started the Remember My Name project in 1994 to create a national registry of names to increase public awareness of domestic violence deaths. Each year during Domestic Violence Awareness Month a new poster is published memorializing those lost to domestic violence. Members can download the 8'x10' version.

Discounts from Oxford University Press

Oxford University Press is a department of the University of Oxford. It furthers the University's objective of excellence in research, scholarship, and education by publishing worldwide. We publish for all audiences–from pre-school to secondary level schoolchildren; students to academics; general readers to researchers; individuals to institutions. The Interpersonal Violence Series disseminates the latest research and empirically supported prevention and intervention techniques for violence of all types. Series books may be empirical or theoretical, and may focus on victims, perpetrators, or larger systems like the media and the courts, but all provide researchers, clinicians, advocates, and policy-makers with cutting-edge material on the problem of violence.
