Youth Who Cause Harm: Integrating Strategies of Support Toward Anti-Violence

1:00pm – 2:30pm EDT

Members Register Here

Session Description

Speaker: Dr. Alisha Guthery

In the United States, approximately 19% of adolescents experience offline dating violence (ADV), and approximately the same percentage of teens experience online dating violence . This violence has serious health consequences, including eating disorders, depression, a nxiety, suicidal ideation, and homicide. Further, ADV is a predictor of adult-perpetrated re lational violence. Emerging research on ADV equips anti-violence professionals with a uni que opportunity for intervention that may assist in the eradication of later perpetrated viol ence, particularly if efforts are directed toward youth offenders. This discussion will focus on understanding the primary components of ADV, the neurobiological underpinnings for youth perpetration, and what you can do about it. This discussion will provide you with sp ecific interventions for youth, along with tools for advocating within your community.
